## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters, potential EA clients, and technical collaborators landing on Tarun's portfolio. Viewed on desktop primarily. The job to be done: assess whether Tarun is credible, interesting, and worth reaching out to — within 30 seconds.

### Brand Personality
**measured · operational · deliberate**

Not a hacker. Not a creative. An executor who also builds. The interface should feel like a well-run studio — confident, editorial, nothing wasted.

### Aesthetic Direction
**hellomonday-grade editorial.** Near-pure white background. Darker Grotesque 900wt for all display text — large, tight-tracked, no italic. Literata for body reading. Warm amber accent (`var(--amber)`) used only as a single warmth note. Near-black ink (#1a1a1a range).

Motion: custom lerp-following ring cursor (38px → 66px on hover), page fade-in on load, 56px translateY reveals with 1s spring easing, nav underline-draw on hover. Everything feels physically weighted, not springy or bouncy.

Anti-references: italic display fonts, glassmorphism, neon accents, gradient text, border-left accent stripes.

### Design Principles
1. **Display text is bold, never italic.** Darker Grotesque 900wt at -0.045em tracking creates the editorial authority. Italics are reserved for inline emphasis in body copy only.
2. **White space is active.** Large gaps between sections feel intentional, not empty. The whiteness of the background is part of the design.
3. **Cursor is the signature.** The lerp ring cursor is the first impression on desktop. It must always feel smooth and physically present.
4. **Motion is weighted.** Reveals travel 56px, take 1s, spring-ease. Never bouncy. Never instant.
5. **Hierarchy through scale, not color.** Amber is used once (hero label). Everything else is ink on white at varied opacities.
